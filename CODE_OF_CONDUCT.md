# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as team members pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy team environment.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes
* Focusing on what is best for the team and project
* Showing empathy towards other team members
* Communicating clearly and professionally
* Being punctual for team meetings
* Completing assigned tasks on time
* Asking for help when needed

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
* Missing team meetings without prior notice
* Not responding to team communications in a timely manner
* Taking credit for work done by others
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Team Responsibilities

All team members are responsible for:

* Upholding these standards
* Taking corrective action when witnessing unacceptable behavior
* Communicating concerns to the team or faculty advisor
* Supporting each other's learning and growth
* Ensuring fair distribution of work
* Meeting project deadlines

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

* GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
* Team meetings (in-person and virtual)
* Email and messaging communications
* Any project-related events
* Interactions with project sponsors

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to:

* The team lead
* The faculty advisor
* USF Computer Science Department administration

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All team members are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

## Academic Integrity

This project is subject to USF's Academic Integrity policies. All team members must:

* Properly attribute any external code or resources used
* Not share project code with students outside the team
* Report any academic integrity concerns
* Ensure all work submitted is original or properly cited
